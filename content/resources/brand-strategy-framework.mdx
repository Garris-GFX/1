Brand strategy doesn't need to be long to be useful. The goal is clarity: what you stand for, who you serve, and how you act differently.

Start with three simple planes:

- Purpose: the core reason you exist beyond features and price.
- Promise: the primary benefit customers should expect.
- Personality: the consistent tone and visual vocabulary you use.

Use these planes to make everyday choices: button style, photography, copy tone, pricing page structure. If a decision doesn't support your purpose, question it.

Apply the framework monthly: pick one plane and audit a page or touchpoint. Small, focused improvements compound into a reliable brand experience without a heavy retainer.

Checklist

- Write a one-sentence purpose.
- Define a one-line promise.
- Pick three personality adjectives and enforce them across channels.

This lightweight approach keeps brand work practical and repeatable — exactly what small teams need.
